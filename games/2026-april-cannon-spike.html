About This Game

Cannon Spike — known as Gun Spike in Japan — was developed by Psikyo and published by Capcom in 2000. It's a multidirectional top-down shooter that plays like a love letter to Capcom's roster, dropping iconic characters into fast-paced arcade action against hordes of robots and screen-filling bosses. The game features seven playable characters drawn from across Capcom's franchises: Cammy and Charlie Nash from Street Fighter, Arthur from Ghosts 'n Goblins, Mega Man, B.B. Hood from Darkstalkers, and two original characters — Shiba Shintaro and Simone.

Each character has unique attacks, special moves, and a powerful super attack, giving the short campaign surprising replay value. The game supports two-player co-op, which cranks the chaos up to eleven. As a Dreamcast exclusive that received a limited print run, Cannon Spike has become one of the most sought-after titles for the system, with complete copies regularly fetching high prices. Despite its brevity — a skilled run takes well under an hour — the score-chasing depth and character variety make it a game built to be replayed over and over.

Did You Know?

  • Capcom crossover all-stars — Cannon Spike features playable characters from five different Capcom franchises: Street Fighter (Cammy, Charlie), Ghosts 'n Goblins (Arthur), Mega Man, and Darkstalkers (B.B. Hood). It's one of the earliest Capcom crossover games outside of the Vs. fighting series.
  • Developed by shmup legends — The game was developed by Psikyo, the studio behind classic shoot-'em-ups like Strikers 1945 and Gunbird. Their expertise in bullet patterns and boss design is evident throughout Cannon Spike's intense encounters.
  • One of the most expensive Dreamcast games — Due to its limited print run, complete copies of Cannon Spike regularly sell for $200+ on the collector's market, making it one of the priciest titles in the Dreamcast library.
  • Arthur keeps his armor gimmick — True to Ghosts 'n Goblins tradition, Arthur in Cannon Spike can lose his armor when hit, continuing to fight in his boxers. His moveset also references his lance-throwing abilities from his home series.

Soundtrack

Composed by Masara Nishijima

The Cannon Spike soundtrack delivers high-energy arcade beats that match the game's frantic pace. Pounding techno and rock-infused tracks keep the adrenaline pumping through each boss encounter, while character select themes give each fighter a distinct musical identity. It's a quintessential late-90s arcade soundtrack — loud, fast, and unapologetic.
