Late Spring (1949)

A widowed professor in postwar Tokyo lives contentedly with his devoted adult daughter Noriko. When family and friends begin pressuring Noriko to marry before it's "too late," she resists, perfectly content with caring for her father. Gradually, the father comes to believe he must convince her to accept marriage, even at the cost of his own happiness and companionship. Ozu's masterful restraint transforms this simple domestic premise into one of cinema's most deeply moving meditations on family, duty, and the bittersweet passage of time.

DirectorYasujirō Ozu
Year1949
Runtime108 min
GenreDrama (Shomin-geki)
StarringSetsuko Hara, Chishū Ryū, Yumeji Tsukioka, Haruko Sugimura
AwardsMainichi Film Awards (Best Film, Director, Screenplay, Actress); topped Kinema Junpo poll
StreamCriterion Channel, Tubi (free), Plex (free), Prime Video

Michael Clayton (2007)

Michael Clayton is the top "fixer" at one of New York's largest corporate law firms, the man called in to clean up the messes others can't handle. When the firm's top litigator Arthur Edens suffers a breakdown in the middle of a multibillion-dollar chemical company class action lawsuit, Clayton is sent to rein him in. But as he digs deeper, Clayton discovers that Edens may have stumbled onto a deadly truth the chemical company will do anything to bury. Tony Gilroy's directorial debut is a taut, meticulously crafted legal thriller anchored by George Clooney's career-best performance.

DirectorTony Gilroy
Year2007
Runtime119 min
GenreLegal Thriller
StarringGeorge Clooney, Tom Wilkinson, Tilda Swinton, Sydney Pollack
Awards7 Oscar nominations incl. Best Picture; Tilda Swinton won Best Supporting Actress
StreamPrime Video (rent), Apple TV (rent), Fandango at Home (rent)
